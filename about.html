About 1001 McKinney

1001 McKinney is a historic downtown Houston office space that seamlessly integrates timeless architecture with today's first-class working amenities.

🐝

A Piece of Houston History

Our building wasn't always called 1001 McKinney. Our origins date back nearly 100 years to 1939, when James A. Elkins, cofounder of international law firm Vinson & Elkins, appointed renowned architect Alfred C. Finn to design a building to house Houston's City National Bank — which opened its doors nearly eight years after the concept was conceived, in 1947. By the time of completion, the City National Bank building was the first office building built in Downtown after World War II and was the second tallest building in Houston.

When creating 1001 McKinney, Finn — who designed many of Houston's iconic landmarks such as The Rice in Downtown and the San Jacinto Monument in Baytown — was inspired by Art Moderne, an architectural style in-vogue at the time that emphasized simple, streamlined shapes and curved lines.

Today, much of the building's original Art Moderne design still exists: the marble walls, the geometric designs, the grand ceiling detail, the three-story red marble columns, and so much more. Nevertheless, 1001 McKinney is geared with a myriad of contemporary amenities, including secure key card access, tunnel access, 24/7 security, an on-site gym and a full-service car detailer in the parking garage.

The Honeybee

Of all the various details that remain from Finn's original design, hands-down we have a favorite: the honeybee. Aloft the building's Main Street entry sits an aluminum frame embossed with the icon of a bee. The bee is symbolic of diligence, work, and good order, and served as a perfect icon for the original City National Bank as well as the 1001 McKinney you see today.

We loved the symbol and its meaning so much that we incorporated the bee throughout the 1001 McKinney brand, including our logo. At 1001 McKinney, we pride ourselves on being hard-working, nimble, fast and efficient — just like that of a bee.
